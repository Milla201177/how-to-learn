Main Problems in Learning

Neither school nor university teaches us how to study effectively. We prepare for exams and memorize information. We practice solving repetitive tasks to pass tests, but often this does not lead to real understanding. Rote memorization fades quickly and brings little practical value.

Conclusion: learning itself is a skill, yet very few places actually teach it. So what can we do about it?

Specific techniques and exercises can transform the learning process, making it both effective and engaging. These same methods are used in Practicum’s educational approach.

Learning Techniques

Five practices by Barbara Oakley

  • Two Types of Attention

    Deep understanding appears when focused and diffuse thinking are alternated.

  • Recall

    Recalling learned material helps connect scattered pieces of memory.

  • Interleaving

    Study multiple skills simultaneously — they reinforce one another.

  • Questions

    While listening to a teacher, come up with meaningful questions that genuinely interest you.

  • Illusion of Competence

    Repeat it, write it down, explain it to a friend — otherwise you may only think you understand the topic.

Barbara Oakley’s Story

Barbara struggled with mathematics from childhood. She considered herself a complete humanities person — and not a particularly talented one. While serving in the army, she studied Russian to receive additional pay and succeeded so well that she was promoted to leadership positions. However, career advancement required mathematics exams. That’s when Barbara developed her own approach to technical subjects. She discovered that if something feels difficult, the knowledge earned through effort often becomes deeper and more lasting than for those who understand everything immediately.
